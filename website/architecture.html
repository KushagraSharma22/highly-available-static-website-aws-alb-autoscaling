Reference Architecture

Highly Available 3-Tier Flow

Traffic enters through DNS and a load balancer, fans out across an auto-scaling compute layer in two Availability Zones, and lands on a managed, Multi-AZ database — all observed continuously.

Users

Requests arrive globally from browsers, mobile apps, and API clients.

Amazon Route 53

Managed DNS resolves the domain and performs health-check-based failover routing.

Application Load Balancer

Distributes incoming traffic evenly across healthy targets in every Availability Zone.

Auto Scaling Group

Watches demand and launches or terminates instances automatically to match load.

EC2 Instance 1

Availability Zone A

EC2 Instance 2

Availability Zone B

Amazon RDS (Multi-AZ)

A managed relational database with a synchronously replicated standby for automatic failover.

Amazon CloudWatch

Collects metrics and logs from every layer, triggering scaling policies and alarms.

Amazon SNS

Fans out alarm notifications to the engineering team by email and SMS in real time.

Design Principles

What makes this architecture "highly available"

No single point of failure

Every tier is duplicated across at least two Availability Zones, so the loss of one zone does not take the app down.

Elastic by default

The Auto Scaling Group reacts to CloudWatch metrics, adding capacity ahead of demand and removing it once traffic subsides.

Defense in depth

Security Groups, a private VPC, and IAM roles restrict access at the network and identity layers simultaneously.
